Trauma-Informed Youth Work

Understanding & Healing
Developmental Trauma.

Trauma is not a diagnosis. It is a neurobiological response to unsafe conditions. Learn to recognise it, understand how it manifests, and respond with the structural support that leads to genuine healing.

The Course

A

This is not a clinical course. It is a practical deep-dive into how trauma shows up in young people, how professionals can recognise it, and how to build environments and relationships that heal rather than re-traumatise.

01
Format
8 comprehensive modules. Live case study discussions. Practical implementation tools.
02
Duration
7–8 weeks. 4–5 hours per week. In-depth and nuanced.
03
Who It's For
Teachers, youth workers, counsellors, social workers, anyone working with young people who've experienced trauma.
04
Outcomes
Deep understanding of trauma responses. Ability to recognise patterns. Framework for trauma-informed responses. Self-awareness as a trauma professional.

What You'll Learn

B

Understanding Trauma

  • Types of trauma: acute, complex, developmental, intergenerational
  • Neurobiology of trauma responses
  • Trauma and attachment, behaviour, learning
  • The role of safety, trust, and predictability

Trauma-Informed Practice

  • Recognising trauma responses in real settings
  • How systems can re-traumatise (and how to prevent it)
  • Building safety and predictability
  • Self-care and professional boundaries
  • When to refer and collaborative work

Curriculum

C

Module 1: Trauma 101
Types of trauma. The neurobiology. Why young people who've experienced trauma behave differently.
Module 2: Developmental Trauma
Impact during critical periods. Attachment disruption. Long-term consequences.
Module 3: Recognition & Assessment
Spotting trauma in the classroom, youth centre, home. Common presentations. Mimic vs. actual trauma.
Module 4: Creating Safety
Predictability, structure, consistency. How environment supports trauma healing. Building trust with traumatised young people.
Module 5: Trauma-Informed Relationships
Boundaries that protect. Co-regulation. Responding to dysregulation without retraumatising.
Module 6–7: Real Cases & Complex Presentations
Working through scenarios. Multiple traumas. When systems fail. Accountability and repair.
Module 8: Self-Care & Sustainability
Vicarious trauma and burnout. Building resilience as a professional. Ongoing learning and support.
