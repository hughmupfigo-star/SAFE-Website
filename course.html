A 6–8 week online course for youth workers, teachers, community leaders, and parents. Learn evidence-based frameworks to recognise at-risk young people before crisis strikes, build sustainable resilience structures, and create spaces where prevention is the default.

The Course

A

This course is built on the same principles that drive SAFE: the belief that structure, transparency, and intentional design can transform how we support young people. Over eight weeks, you'll move from framework understanding to real-world application — designing prevention systems that work in your school, youth centre, or team.

What You'll Learn

B

Recognition & Assessment

  • Early warning signs: emotional, behavioural, and structural indicators of risk
  • Trauma-informed assessment: moving beyond diagnosis to understanding context
  • The "invisible structure" principle: why environment matters as much as individual support
  • Building your team's confidence to spot what matters

Systems & Design

  • Prevention frameworks: from theory to practice in real spaces
  • Designing rooms, routines, and relationships that prevent crisis
  • The role of transparency: why young people respond to honest, structured environments
  • Scaling prevention: adapting principles to schools, youth centres, and community spaces

Implementation

  • Audit your current space: what's working, what's missing
  • Build a prevention roadmap: actionable steps you can take immediately
  • Create accountability structures: how to measure if prevention is actually happening
  • Lead your team through change: managing resistance and sustaining momentum

Resilience & Sustainability

  • The neuroscience of resilience: what the brain actually needs
  • Building capacity in young people (not dependence on professionals)
  • Self-regulation as a skill, not a diagnosis
  • Sustaining prevention work: avoiding burnout and maintaining fidelity

Curriculum

C

Module 1: The Problem & The Promise
Why crisis intervention models are failing. The prevention-first philosophy. What the data actually shows about early intervention. Why young people are not the problem — the systems around them are.
Module 2: Recognition — The Invisible Signs
How to spot risk before it becomes crisis. Emotional indicators, behavioural patterns, environmental red flags. How to build a culture of noticing in your team. Case studies from real youth work settings.
Module 3: Understanding Context & Trauma
Trauma-informed practice: beyond diagnosis. The role of poverty, injustice, and systemic harm. Why "resilience" frameworks often miss the structural problem. Rethinking what we ask of young people.
Module 4: Designing Prevention Systems
The SAFE Hub framework: intentional design across body, mind, voice, identity. How structure prevents crisis. The invisible load-bearing walls of prevention. Scaling design: from a single room to a whole building to a whole system.
Module 5: Live Workshop — Audit Your Space
Walk through your current environment (school, centre, team). What's working? What's missing? Where do young people fall through the cracks? Peer feedback. Honest conversation about real constraints.
Module 6: Building Your Prevention Roadmap
From diagnosis to action. Small moves that compound. Regulatory compliance and human development — why they're not opposites. Managing stakeholders. Budget and resource strategies for prevention in resource-poor settings.
Module 7: Measurement & Accountability
How do you know if prevention is actually working? Outcome measurement beyond metrics. Tracking what matters: reoffending, mental health, school attendance, sense of belonging. Building feedback loops with your team and the young people you serve.
Module 8: Live Workshop — Implementation & Scaling
Present your prevention roadmap. Feedback from facilitators and peers. Anticipate blockers. Sustaining momentum. Committing to action. Long-term support and community for ongoing learning.

Questions

G

What's the time commitment?
3–4 hours per week. Video modules are designed for busy professionals — watch at your own pace, take notes, download worksheets. The live workshops are 90 minutes each and scheduled in advance so you can plan around them.
Do I need prior experience in youth work?
No. This course is designed for anyone responsible for young people's development — youth workers, teachers, school leaders, parents, community organisers. If you work with young people and want to prevent crisis before it happens, this is for you.
Can my whole team do this together?
Yes. Group rates are available for teams of 5 or more. Many schools and youth centres take the course together so they can build their prevention roadmap as a unified group. Contact us to discuss team options.
What happens after the course ends?
You keep lifetime access to all materials and templates. You're part of an ongoing community of practice — optional monthly calls with other participants. Your prevention roadmap becomes your implementation guide. You'll have the frameworks to build on this for years to come.
Is this accredited?
Not formally accredited, but completion is recognised and valued by practitioners. The focus is on practical application, not credentials. You'll have proof of completion and a roadmap that speaks louder than any certificate.
Can I get a refund?
Yes. 14-day full refund if the course isn't what you expected. No questions asked. We want you enrolled because you're committed, not because you feel trapped.
